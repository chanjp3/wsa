Block 03 On the books · The owner's ledger

Charter flips
the sign.

Flying it yourself costs nearly $600K a year.

At 150 owner-flown hours, fixed costs, crew, programs, fuel, and the management fee total an estimated $598,308 — and an aircraft that just sits still burns $469,373.

Flying it pays you instead.

Put 300 charter hours on the calendar and the 85% aircraft allocation covers every fixed cost, every flight hour — including your own 50 — and our management fee, with roughly $92K left over.

You stay in control.

Your schedule always comes first. You set blackout dates and minimum notice, and approve availability before anything is booked.
